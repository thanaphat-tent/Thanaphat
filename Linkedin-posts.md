### AI SEO transformation fails at the alignment layer, not the tactics layer

AI SEO transformation fails at the alignment layer, not the tactics layer.

Right now, teams are panic-testing short-term tactics rather than reorienting the whole ship for better long-term outcomes.

The proof? In the recent Growth Memo reader survey, 84% of practitioners said they feel their current LLM visibility measurement approach is inaccurate.

You can see this misalignment happening in real-time when:
1. Executives ask for increased traffic in a growing zero-click environment.
2. One stakeholder wants "rankings in ChatGPT," another wants brand mentions, and no one agrees on how to measure value.
3. Different teams (SEO, content, PR, product) run experiments in their silos because there is no explicit ownership.

AI SEO is not just an algorithm update; it is a search product change and a user behavior movement. Because of this, our roles have to evolve. We have to elevate from being channel executors to organizational translators.

If you can't explain your AI SEO mandate to leadership in one sentence, you aren't ready to execute.

## Matt Diggity — Linkedin-posts

### AI Overviews appear on 20%+ of US keywords (and steal clicks)

AI Overviews now appear on **20%+ of all US keywords**.

And if you rank **#1** on a SERP with an AI Overview? Your clicks drop by **34.5% on average**.

You’re not losing rankings. You’re losing attention to Google’s own answer layer.

That changes the job:
- **Old goal**: win position #1
- **New goal**: become the source AI chooses to quote (and the brand users remember)

Here’s how to increase the odds that AI cites **your** content instead of your competitors’.

#### 1/ Write direct, extractable answers

AI doesn’t “read” your whole page. It **extracts passages**.

Make each section able to stand alone:
- Put a **bold, ~50-word answer** directly under each heading
- Use the format: **“[Query] is [Answer].”**
- Keep intros short; move context **after** the answer
- Prefer crisp statements over clever copy

**Mini-template you can reuse**
- **Definition**: “[Term] is [definition]…”
- **How it works**: “[Process] works by [steps/logic]…”
- **Recommendation**: “For [situation], the best option is [choice] because [reason]…”

#### 2/ Increase your fact density

AI systems tend to prioritize content packed with **unique, verifiable data points**.

Ways to do that fast:
- Add **original stats** (even small samples are better than none if labeled clearly)
- Include **case studies** with specific numbers (baseline → change → outcome)
- Use **comparison tables** (models love structured data)
- Build FAQ sections that mirror **People Also Ask** style questions
- Add definition boxes, step-by-step lists, and “when to use / when not to use” blocks

Generic content gets paraphrased. Fact-dense content gets cited.

#### 3/ Use schema markup (strategically)

Schema helps machines parse what your page *is* and where the key answers live.

High-impact schema to standardize:
- **Article**: every blog post
- **FAQPage**: Q&A content (real questions, real answers)
- **HowTo**: tutorials and processes
- **Organization**: About / brand entity signals
- **Product**: e-commerce (and product-led SaaS pages when applicable)

Also: check `robots.txt`. Make sure you’re not blocking key crawlers (where relevant to your strategy), like **GPTBot**, **ClaudeBot**, or **PerplexityBot**.

#### 4/ Keep content fresh (and make freshness visible)

Recency is often a tiebreaker when AI selects sources.

A strong guide from 2024 with no updates can lose to a decent 2026 article.

Do this:
- Add a visible **“Last updated”** timestamp near the top
- Refresh stats/examples **quarterly**
- Update your **cornerstone pages first** (they’re the most likely to be referenced)
- Keep a changelog section for major edits (helps you update with intent)

#### 5/ Measure what matters (not just rankings)

Traditional SEO metrics miss the new reality. Track:
- **AI citation frequency**: how often you’re referenced in AI answers
- **Share of voice**: mentions vs competitors across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity
- **Citation sentiment**: is AI representing you accurately?
- **AI-referred traffic**: visits from AI search/assistants (track via GA4 attribution + UTMs where possible)

Tools like **Ahrefs Brand Radar** now track **260M+ monthly prompts**. Use them to quantify visibility shifts.

**Question**: What’s your first move when a money page starts getting an AI Overview above it?

## Aleyda Solís — Linkedin-posts

### Where AI Search Sends Traffic: 10-Market Patterns for Your Global AI Search Strategy

✈️ **Where AI Search Sends Traffic: 10-Market Patterns for Your Global AI Search Strategy** 👇 The outcome of my latest International AI search traffic analysis:

There’s a comfortable narrative around AI search right now: AI assistants surface the biggest brands, ecommerce concentrates around marketplaces, finance flows to trusted institutions, travel goes to the major OTAs, and the playbook is to “be a recognized brand.”

That story is partly true. But after going through the top click-producing AI search domains across ecommerce, finance, and travel & tourism in **10 international markets** in March 2026, using Similarweb data, the most useful pattern I can pull from this data is that **AI search isn’t one thing.** The shape of the click-receiving layer changes radically by vertical and by country, and so does the strategy that actually works inside it.

Here’s what the data actually shows.

**Patterns**

- **Pattern 1:** AI search concentration is not the same across verticals — it’s an order of magnitude different.
- **Pattern 2:** AI search traffic often goes to local infrastructure, not global defaults.
- **Pattern 3:** “AI search is growing” is true, but masks meaningful churn.
- **Pattern 4:** “Trust-led finance” is real, but the headline is misread.
- **Pattern 5:** Travel is fragmented at the discovery layer, but more concentrated at the booking and infrastructure layer.

**So what does this actually mean for AI search strategy?**

1. Stop treating AI search as a single optimization problem.
2. Audit local champions before investing in any non-US market.
3. Localization needs to extend into entity presence and structured data, not just translation.
4. Measure AI search movement, not static snapshots.
5. For brands inside concentrated heads, accept the structural reality and play your actual game.
6. Separate AI visibility from traffic ownership and business impact.
