### “Why pay for content?” — attention vs. production

My biggest SEO 🚩 is hearing: "Why should I pay for content? We've got custom prompts for that."

For decades, content creation was expensive. AI changed that. Teams are cutting writers. Publishing 10x more. And seeing visibility fall. Because content is no longer scarce. But attention still is.

Audiences are overwhelmed. Algorithms filter aggressively. Platforms reward engagement.

AI did not make content marketing cheaper. It made attention harder to earn.

The reality is you still need:

🟣 Something to say  
🟣 Packaged to be distribution worthy  
🟣 Clear associations back to the brand  

The bottleneck of marketing has moved from production to attention. Most SEO teams are focussed on the wrong constraint.

### Deleted 60%+ of a real estate blog — clicks climbed

I deleted over 60% of articles from a real estate website. The CMO was nervous for 3 weeks. Then clicks began to climb.

The blog was full of "perfect" SEO content. EEAT compliant. Ranking. Yet, mostly ignored.

Few clicks. No shares. No deeper site engagement. Because it read like ChatGPT wrote it.

These articles were anti-advertisements. They didn't persuade, intrigue, or differentiate. They taught the market that the brand had nothing interesting to say.

It doesn't matter whether it's written by a human or generated by AI. If it's commodity content, where everything has already been said, it doesn't deserve to be published.

When something feels thin or falls flat, the problem is rarely the writing. The fault is the focus. You pointed out the obvious rather than the interesting.

Don't write:

🟣 "How much protein do I need to build muscle?" but "The Dry Chicken Fallacy: Why chasing lean protein is killing your performance"  
🟣 "How do I start investing in real estate?" but "Why 'Rentvesting' is the smartest path to property ownership for Gen Z"  
🟣 "How do I lower CAC" but "How lowering CAC is the fastest way to kill brand salience"  

Stop optimising for the search bar and start optimising for the share buttons.

The goal isn’t to be the top ranked page explaining a concept. It’s to be the brand with a perspective on it.

Winning content closes the distance between information (the what) and insight (the so what). Brand equity is built where standard advice fails.

Before publishing, run the Anonymous Test. Strip away header, footer, sidebar and other template elements. If no one could tell who wrote it, rework it.

This approach creates less content. It also removes excuses for publishing forgettable work.

### Omnichannel SEO — beyond Google alone

SEO isn't just about Google anymore... and it's not just because of ChatGPT.

We've been spoilt. Over a decade of relatively easy, free organic search traffic. That privilege is now slipping away. And I am reminded of the adage: easy come, easy go.

Now we have a choice. Cry over the loss of "our traffic"...That was never actually ours because we don't own it.

Or accept that raw session numbers from google organic was never the point, and refocus on visibility at category entry points.

I call this omnichannel SEO.

Don't give up on Google Search. But don't focus on it alone.

What are you doing to grow your brand on Google Discover, News, Shopping, Lens, Maps, Apps, ChatGPT, Gemini, YouTube and relevant social networks.

Hat tip to Michelle Robbins, MSBA for reemphasising the importance of this again for me today.
